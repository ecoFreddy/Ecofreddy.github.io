Research Overview

My current research integrates process-based models, Earth observation data, and machine learning to better understand and predict ecosystem responses under changing change.

My broader interests include agroecosystem and ecosystem modeling, global change ecology, landscape ecology, remote sensing, and artificial intelligence. My current work aims to link ecological mechanisms with data-driven approaches, including knowledge-guided and interpretable machine learning, to improve predictions of ecosystem productivity, carbon fluxes, water dynamics, and nitrogen processes.

Selected Research Experience

1. Florida Grassland Hybrid Model Development

Role: Project Leader, supervised by Dr. Jiangxiao Qiu

I lead a project that develops a hybrid modeling framework for Florida grassland systems by integrating process-based modeling, Earth observation data, and machine learning. The project aims to improve the prediction of pasture productivity and environmental responses under different soil, climate, and management conditions.

  • Collected and processed soil, weather, and management data, and reformatted these datasets as inputs for process-based ecosystem modeling.
  • Developed a grazing module to better represent the effects of grazing management in APSIM, and adjusted bahiagrass species parameters to better capture the growth characteristics of Florida bahiagrass pastures.
  • Extracted and integrated Earth observations, including remote sensing products, eddy covariance observations, and field measurements, to calibrate and evaluate model performance at the local scale.
  • Aiming to integrate the calibrated process-based model into a knowledge-guided machine learning framework to improve large-scale prediction accuracy while increasing computational efficiency.

2. Apply Landscape Forest Model to Estimate Carbon Accumulation

Role: Undergraduate Project Leader supervised by Liangyuan Cai (蔡良苑), Dr. Shuangliang (梁爽), Dr. Weile Chen (陈伟乐)

This project used the LANDIS-II forest landscape model to evaluate carbon accumulation patterns under different forest planting strategies. The study compared mixed plantations and monocultures to assess how species composition and landscape-level forest structure influence long-term carbon storage.

  • Collected species traits, life-history information, and ecological parameters to support species-level parameterization across different ecoregions.
  • Applied forest landscape modeling to simulate carbon accumulation under alternative plantation designs and species combinations.
  • Analyzed simulated carbon accumulation under mixed plantations and monocultures to evaluate how forest composition influences long-term carbon storage and to provide insights for climate change mitigation.
Publication: Cai, L., Cui, W., Fu,F., Chen, W., Liang, S. Mixed-species plantations sustain long-term, climate-resilient carbon benefits over monocultures in subtropical China. (Manuscript in submission)

3. Field Experiments and Laboratory Analysis

Role: Project Assistant; Lab and Fieldwork Coordinator

I assisted with field experiments and laboratory analyses associated with long-term agroecosystem and environmental monitoring.

  • Assisted with multiple field experiments at the LTAR station, including soil sampling, root sampling, and monthly greenhouse gas sampling.
  • Supported laboratory analyses, including root washing, sorting, and weighing; soil sieving; dry matter measurement; and nutrient analysis.
  • Helped coordinate monthly fieldwork activities by communicating with LTAR collaborators to confirm field sites, availability, sampling schedules, and logistical needs.
  • Presented fieldwork progress and sampling plans to the team, helped organize participation in a way that was reasonable and respectful of members’ schedules, and contributed to cross-group communication and coordination.
  • Managed field equipment and organized collected samples with careful attention to accuracy, responsibility, and sample traceability.
